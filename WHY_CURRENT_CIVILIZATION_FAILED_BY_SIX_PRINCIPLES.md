## 1. Summary

Current civilization did not fail because science and technology were insufficient.

Science advanced. Industry expanded. Cities grew. Information processing, logistics, finance, military systems, and AI all developed.

Yet civilization has moved toward global warming, forest loss, soil degradation, ocean pollution, resource conflict, war, inequality, psychological isolation, and institutional distrust.

The reason is that the Six Principles were missing from the kernel of Civilization OS.

```text
Natural Law was ignored.
Harmony was broken.
Circulation was cut.
Structure was distorted.
Order became runaway control.
Wa was lost.
```

These six absences produced the failure of current civilization.

---

## 2. Civilization Diagnosis by the Six Principles

The Six Principles are the root principles for long-term civilizational survival.

```text
1. Natural Law
2. Harmony
3. Circulation
4. Structure
5. Order
6. Wa
```

A sustainable civilization is one aligned with these six principles.

The failure of current civilization can therefore be explained as violations of these six principles.

---

## 3. Violation of Natural Law

Natural Law refers to the basic conditions of Earth and the biosphere that cannot be changed by human convenience.

Current civilization treated natural law not as a constraint, but as something to overcome.

```text
Water must circulate.
Soil requires microorganisms and organic matter.
Forests support evapotranspiration and water retention.
Oceans circulate heat, oxygen, and nutrients.
The biosphere stabilizes through diversity.
```

Current civilization cleared forests, made soils chemically dependent, drained water, incinerated organic matter, polluted oceans, and treated the atmosphere as a dumping space.

This is a violation of natural law.

Temporary productivity may increase.  
But in the long term, Earth’s cooling, carbon-fixation, and life-support functions collapse.

---

## 4. Destruction of Harmony

Harmony means that different elements do not destroy each other, but strengthen whole-system stability.

Current civilization has been based not on harmony, but on conquest, competition, extraction, and domination.

```text
Humanity vs nature
Economy vs ecosystem
City vs water cycle
Nation vs nation
Religion vs religion
Technology vs biosphere
```

In such a structure, civilization pursues partial victory rather than whole-system stability.

The benefit of one region becomes exploitation of another.  
The profit of one generation becomes the loss of future generations.  
The gain of one company becomes ecological damage.

The absence of harmony expands civilization temporarily, but destabilizes it over the long term.

---

## 5. Disconnection of Circulation

Circulation is the core condition of living systems and civilization systems.

Current civilization has operated through a linear structure:

```text
Extract
Burn
Produce
Consume
Discard
Incinerate
Emit
```

This linear civilization fundamentally contradicts Earth as a circulating planet.

The main circulations disrupted by current civilization include:

```text
Water circulation
Carbon circulation
Nutrient circulation
Microbial circulation
Atmospheric circulation
Ocean circulation
Food circulation
Organic matter circulation
Urban resource circulation
```

When circulation is cut, soil dies, water runs off, cities store heat, oceans lose oxygen, carbon accumulates in the atmosphere, and food systems become unstable.

Global warming is one representative output of this circulation failure.

---

## 6. Structural Defects

Circulation requires structure.

For water to circulate, forests, soil, rivers, wetlands, groundwater, and oceans are needed.  
For cities to cool, waterways, greenery, wind paths, water-retentive structures, and waste-heat design are needed.  
For food to circulate, soil, microorganisms, humus, crops, and return of food residues are needed.

Current civilization has destroyed the structures that sustain circulation.

```text
Wetlands were filled.
Rivers were straightened.
Groundwater recharge was weakened.
Cities were asphalted.
Forests were converted into monocultures.
Agriculture became chemically dependent.
Organic matter was incinerated instead of returned to soil.
```

If structure is broken, circulation cannot return.

The failure of current civilization is therefore both a philosophical failure and a structural design failure.

---

## 7. Runaway Order

Order should prevent collapse and maintain circulation and harmony.

In current civilization, however, order shifted toward domination, control, competition, and hierarchy.

```text
State order → military rivalry
Market order → short-term profit
Corporate order → shareholder primacy
Technological order → efficiency and management
Information order → surveillance and manipulation
```

Order is necessary.  
But order separated from the Six Principles does not protect natural law, harmony, or circulation. It protects and extends the existing system.

As a result, destructive economic activity can be legalized, ecological destruction expands if profitable, and short-term gain takes priority over future generations.

This is not the absence of order. It is the failure of the direction of order.

---

## 8. Loss of Wa

Wa is the civilizational intention at the summit of the Six Principles.

Wa does not mean hiding conflict.  
It means integrating differences toward the harmony of the whole without erasing them.

Current civilization chose division rather than Wa.

```text
Us and not-us
Winners and losers
Developed and developing countries
Humanity and nature
Economy and environment
Science and spirit
AI and humans
```

Without Wa, intelligence and technology move toward domination.

Without Wa, AI becomes a tool of surveillance and manipulation.  
Without Wa, science becomes a tool of domination over nature.  
Without Wa, the economy becomes a system that takes from others and future generations.

The greatest failure of current civilization was that it did not make Wa the objective function of Civilization OS.

    ↓

## 10. Conceptual Stability Model

Civilization stability can be conceptually expressed as alignment with the Six Principles:

```text
Civilization Stability
= Natural Law
× Harmony
× Circulation
× Structure
× Order
× Wa
```

The important point is multiplication, not addition.

If one principle collapses, the stability of the whole system collapses with it.

A civilization may have advanced structure and order, but without Wa it becomes a control civilization.  
A civilization may have advanced technology, but without circulation it moves toward resource depletion.  
A civilization may have a large economy, but if it violates natural law, it collapses.

## 12. Conclusion

Current civilization did not fail because of insufficient science and technology.

It failed because the Six Principles were not placed at the core of Civilization OS.

It ignored Natural Law, destroyed Harmony, cut Circulation, distorted Structure, turned Order into runaway control, and lost Wa.

As a result, global warming, resource conflict, social division, war, inequality, and ecosystem collapse emerged.

Therefore, the next Civilization OS must be redesigned with the Six Principles as its kernel.

```text
Follow Natural Law.
Increase Harmony.
Restore Circulation.
Organize Structure.
Stabilize Order.
Move toward Wa.
```

This is the minimum condition for sustainable civilization.
