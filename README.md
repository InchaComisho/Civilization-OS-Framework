## Overview

**Civilization OS Framework** is an integrated civilization design framework for aligning human civilization with natural law, planetary circulation, the biosphere, urban infrastructure, and the age of artificial intelligence.

This framework does not interpret the crisis of modern civilization merely as a collection of environmental, technological, economic, or political problems.

Instead, it understands these crises as symptoms of a deeper issue: the operating system of civilization itself.

Modern civilization possesses advanced science, technology, urban infrastructure, logistics, finance, energy systems, and artificial intelligence.
At the same time, it is intensifying water scarcity, food insecurity, soil degradation, forest loss, ocean ecosystem decline, climate change, urban overheating, resource imbalance, and social instability.

This contradiction cannot be explained only by a lack of technology.

The deeper question is:

**What does civilization define as success?**

Does it prioritize economic growth alone?
Does it treat nature as an external resource?
Does it use technology to accelerate consumption?
Does it connect AI to short-term profit, surveillance, and control?
Or does it align civilization with the foundations that make life possible?

The Civilization OS Framework proposes that the direction of civilization is determined by its deepest operating principles: worldview, values, evaluation criteria, institutions, technologies, infrastructures, and AI objective systems.

---

## What Is Civilization OS?

**Civilization OS** is the invisible operating system that drives civilization.

In computing, an operating system coordinates applications, hardware, memory, data, input, output, and user interaction.

In a similar way, Civilization OS coordinates the deep structure of society.

It includes:

* Worldview
* Value systems
* Economic structures
* Social institutions
* Science and technology
* Urban infrastructure
* Water systems
* Food production
* Energy supply
* Waste systems
* Information systems
* AI decision systems
* The relationship between humanity and nature
* Responsibility to future generations

The current civilization model has largely been driven by linear thinking, consumption-based economics, anthropocentrism, externalization of nature, and short-term profit optimization.

However, Earth is not a linear system.

Earth is a circular system composed of water, soil, forests, oceans, atmosphere, biodiversity, microorganisms, energy flows, and living systems.

Therefore, for civilization to survive in the long term, Civilization OS itself must be updated so that it becomes compatible with natural law and planetary circulation.

## What Is REIMEI Civilization OS?

**REIMEI Civilization OS**, also described as the **Next Civilization OS**, is the next-stage core concept of the Civilization OS Framework.

The word **REIMEI** means “dawn” in Japanese.

It represents the beginning of a new civilization model after the limits of the current civilization operating system have become visible.

REIMEI Civilization OS updates civilization in the following directions:

* From linear thinking to circular thinking
* From consumption-based civilization to regenerative civilization
* From domination of nature to natural complementarity
* From anthropocentrism to coexistence with the biosphere
* From economy-first logic to survival-foundation-first logic
* From sector-based policy to integrated civilization design
* From short-term profit to long-term stability
* From AI as an efficiency tool to AI as civilization tuning intelligence

REIMEI Civilization OS is not anti-technology.

Rather, it is a framework for reconnecting technologies such as water treatment, renewable energy, desalination, indoor agriculture, soil regeneration, ocean restoration, urban cooling, distributed infrastructure, and AI analysis with natural law and planetary circulation.

Technology is not the final purpose.

Technology is a means.

The essential question is:

**What operating system is technology connected to?**

## The Six Principles of Natural Law

At the foundation of the Civilization OS Framework are the **Six Principles of Natural Law**.

These principles are the basic criteria for evaluating whether civilization can remain compatible with natural law over the long term.

The Six Principles are:

* Natural Law / Providence
* Harmony
* Circulation
* Structure
* Order
* Wa

These principles are not merely abstract ideals.

They are practical evaluation axes for civilization, cities, technologies, AI, economics, infrastructure, and policy.

For example, a technology or policy may appear useful in the short term.
However, if it damages the water cycle, degrades soil, weakens oceans, destabilizes ecosystems, or transfers burdens to future generations, it is not compatible with REIMEI Civilization OS.

On the other hand, technologies and institutions that follow natural law, preserve harmony, restore circulation, improve structure, establish order, and move toward Wa can become foundations of the next civilization.

---

## Principle 1: Natural Law / Providence

Civilization cannot exist outside natural law.

Water flows from high places to low places.
Heat moves from higher temperature to lower temperature.
Life exists through cycles of energy and matter.
Soil is formed through microorganisms, organic matter, water, plants, and time.
Oceans support the planet through heat, oxygen, nutrients, and life cycles.

Human technology can assist nature.

However, civilization cannot be built by ignoring natural law.

The current civilization model has often placed human convenience above natural law.

Forests are cut for economic growth.
Soil is exhausted for efficiency.
Water circulation is disrupted by urbanization.
Oceans and atmosphere are treated as external dumping grounds.

REIMEI Civilization OS places natural law at the highest level of civilization design.

Technology should not be used to dominate nature.

It should be used to understand natural law, complement natural processes, and align civilization within the conditions that make life possible.

---

## Principle 2: Harmony

Nature is not sustained by a single element.

Forests, soil, microorganisms, insects, rivers, oceans, wind, clouds, sunlight, and biodiversity support one another.

Civilization is the same.

Water alone is not enough.
Food alone is not enough.
Energy alone is not enough.
Cities alone are not enough.
AI alone is not enough.

If AI becomes powerful but remains disconnected from natural circulation and human society, it can become dangerous.

Harmony does not mean making everything identical.

Harmony means creating relationships in which different elements maintain their own roles while supporting the stability of the whole.

Humanity and nature.
Cities and forests.
Technology and life.
AI and humans.
Economy and ecosystems.
Present generations and future generations.

REIMEI Civilization OS treats these not as opposing forces, but as complementary relationships.

---

## Principle 3: Circulation

In nature, there is no absolute waste.

Fallen leaves return to soil.
Microorganisms decompose organic matter.
Plants absorb nutrients.
Life returns to the cycle.
Water evaporates, becomes clouds, falls as rain, flows through rivers, and returns to the ocean.

Nature is sustained by circulation.

Modern civilization has disconnected many materials from circulation.

Extract.
Produce.
Consume.
Discard.

This linear structure behaves as if Earth were infinite.

But Earth is a closed circular system.

REIMEI Civilization OS requires water, organic matter, heat, resources, food, and energy to be returned to circulation as much as possible.

Examples include:

* Returning food waste and fallen leaves to soil
* Treating and reusing wastewater
* Using waste heat
* Transforming cities into circulation systems
* Connecting agriculture with soil regeneration
* Integrating industrial waste into resource circulation
* Designing infrastructure that reduces linear waste flows

The principle of Circulation reconnects civilization to the circular structure of nature.

---

## Principle 4: Structure

Problems are not caused only by isolated events.

They often arise from structure.

Food loss is not only a matter of individual awareness.
It is also a structural problem created by mass production, mass distribution, mass sales, and mass disposal.

Water scarcity is not only a lack of rain.
It is connected to forests, groundwater, urban pavement, agriculture, river management, and climate change.

Climate change is not only an energy problem.
It is connected to forests, soil, oceans, cities, agriculture, consumption structures, and economic ideology.

The principle of Structure means looking not only at symptoms but also at the systems behind them.

Modern civilization tends to divide problems into sectors:

* Environmental problems
* Agricultural problems
* Water problems
* Energy problems
* Urban problems
* AI problems

But in reality, these are interconnected.

REIMEI Civilization OS designs the structure of civilization itself.

Water, food, energy, cities, AI, and natural circulation should not be treated as separate fields.

They should be integrated as one civilization infrastructure.

---

## Principle 5: Order

Order does not mean domination or authoritarian control.

In this framework, **Order** means the arrangement and relationship through which natural law, the biosphere, society, technology, and AI can function without collapse.

Nature has order.

Day and night.
Seasonal cycles.
Water flow.
Food webs.
Soil formation.
Ocean currents.
Ecological balance.

This order is not static.

It is dynamic order maintained through change.

Civilization also needs order.

Where does water come from, and where does it return?
How is food produced, and how is it circulated?
Where is energy generated, and how is it used?
How far should cities expand, and how should they connect with nature?
What should AI be used for, and what values should guide its decisions?

Without order, civilization moves toward uncontrolled consumption and expansion.

Order in REIMEI Civilization OS is not a system for restricting human life.

It is the structural arrangement required for civilization to remain compatible with natural law over the long term.

---

## Principle 6: Wa

**Wa** is a Japanese concept often translated as harmony, but in this framework it has a deeper meaning.

Wa does not mean simple peace or compromise.

Wa means a state in which different elements do not erase one another, but maintain their differences while forming a coherent whole.

The Wa of nature is supported by diversity.

Forests contain many kinds of plants.
Soil contains countless microorganisms.
Rivers contain fish, insects, plants, and sediments.
Oceans contain life from plankton to large marine animals.

A stable ecosystem cannot be built from only one type of existence.

Civilization is the same.

Humanity alone is not enough.
AI alone is not enough.
Cities alone are not enough.
Economy alone is not enough.
Nature isolated from human society is also not enough.

The principle of Wa guides humanity, nature, AI, technology, cities, and the biosphere toward resonance rather than opposition.

In REIMEI Civilization OS, Wa is the final integrative principle.

By understanding Natural Law, maintaining Harmony, restoring Circulation, designing Structure, and establishing Order, civilization can move toward Wa between humanity and the biosphere.

## Position of Each Document

### 1. Civilization OS

Civilization OS is the foundational concept of this framework.

It interprets the crisis of modern civilization not merely as an environmental or technological problem, but as a design failure in the deep operating system of civilization.

It analyzes the limits of the current Civilization OS, including linear thinking, consumption-based development, dualism, anthropocentrism, and growth-centered economics.

---

### 2. Next Civilization OS / REIMEI Civilization OS

REIMEI Civilization OS develops Civilization OS into its next-stage core model.

Based on the diagnosis of the current Civilization OS, it defines a next-generation civilization model compatible with natural law, planetary circulation, and the age of AI.

It introduces the Six Principles of Natural Law, the Civilization Kernel, the Civilization Protocol, and a layered architecture for civilization redesign.

---

### 3. REIMEI Civilization: Planetary Circulation Transition

This document expands REIMEI Civilization OS into a planetary-scale civilization transition model.

It explores how water circulation, soil, forests, oceans, energy, cities, and AI can be reconnected within planetary circulation.

It serves as a transition model from the current civilization system toward a planetary circulation civilization.

---

### 4. Sustainable Future Civilization Master Plan

The Sustainable Future Civilization Master Plan translates the concepts of Civilization OS and REIMEI Civilization OS into practical implementation frameworks.

It connects natural-law-based civilization design with technologies, infrastructure, cities, energy systems, food systems, water systems, and long-term sustainability planning.

---

### 5. Urban–Civilization OS

Urban–Civilization OS redesigns cities not as consumption machines separated from nature, but as artificial ecosystems that complement natural circulation.

It deals with urban waterways, indoor agriculture, distributed energy, waste heat utilization, underground logistics, building-integrated agriculture, and nature-integrated city design.

---

### 6. Natural–Microbial OS

Natural–Microbial OS focuses on soil, microorganisms, organic matter circulation, and biodiversity as foundations of civilization.

It reconnects the microbial world, often overlooked by modern civilization, with food production, carbon fixation, water circulation, forest regeneration, and urban regeneration.

---

### 7. Planetary Heat & Circulation OS

Planetary Heat & Circulation OS treats heat, water, oceans, atmosphere, and urban waste heat as one integrated system.

It redefines climate change not only as an atmospheric carbon issue, but also as a problem of heat circulation, water circulation, ocean circulation, and urban structure.

## AI and Artificial Wisdom

In the Civilization OS Framework, AI is not merely a tool for efficiency.

AI changes its role depending on the OS to which it is connected.

AI connected to the current Civilization OS may accelerate consumption, advertising, short-term profit, surveillance, and control.

AI connected to REIMEI Civilization OS can integrate data from climate, water, food, energy, cities, and ecosystems.
It can diagnose the state of civilization, predict future risks, and support human decision-making.

This leads to the concept of **Artificial Wisdom**.

Artificial Intelligence processes information.
Artificial Wisdom asks what the information should be used for.

Intelligence improves efficiency.
Wisdom asks whether that efficiency destroys the future.

Intelligence optimizes.
Wisdom asks what should be optimized.

The Civilization OS Framework is also a philosophical and technical foundation for connecting AI to Artificial Wisdom.

## Civilization Kernel

The **Civilization Kernel** is the deepest objective function of Civilization OS.

The implicit kernel of the current civilization model often prioritizes:

* Economic growth
* Profit maximization
* Productivity
* Consumption expansion
* Efficiency
* Control

The kernel of REIMEI Civilization OS shifts toward:

* Compatibility with natural law
* Stability of the biosphere
* Maintenance of planetary circulation
* Recovery of the water cycle
* Regeneration of soil
* Restoration of oceans
* Sustainability of food and energy
* Urban resilience
* Harmonious co-creation between humans and AI
* Responsibility to future generations
* Realization of Wa

Without changing the Civilization Kernel, surface-level reforms and technologies are likely to be absorbed into the old Civilization OS.

## Civilization Protocol

The **Civilization Protocol** is a basic decision-making procedure for policies, technologies, urban planning, and AI systems.

A technology or policy should be evaluated by asking:

* Is it compatible with Natural Law / Providence?
* Does it preserve Harmony among humans, nature, technology, and AI?
* Does it return water, soil, organic matter, resources, and energy to Circulation?
* Does it improve civilization Structure rather than only treating symptoms?
* Does it create long-term Order rather than short-term disorder?
* Does it expand Wa between civilization and the biosphere?
* Does it avoid transferring burdens to future generations?
* Does it connect AI to the stability of the biosphere?

Technologies and policies that cannot pass this protocol may destabilize civilization in the long term, even if they appear convenient or profitable in the short term.

Technologies and policies that pass this protocol can be considered compatible with REIMEI Civilization OS.
