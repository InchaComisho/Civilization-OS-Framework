## 1. Summary

The direction of civilization is determined by philosophy.

Science and technology are not inherently good or evil.

The decisive question is which philosophy, value system, and Civilization OS they are connected to.

Modern civilization possesses advanced science and technology, yet it has expanded global warming, forest loss, soil degradation, ocean pollution, resource conflicts, war, inequality, and psychological isolation.

This did not happen because technology was insufficient.

It happened because the underlying Civilization OS has been shaped by dualism, linear thinking, short-term profit, consumption civilization, externalization of nature, and division between “us” and “not us.”

If the foundation of civilization had been harmony, circulation, coexistence, and alignment with natural law, the use of the same science and technology would have been different.

Thus, modern civilizational crisis, including global warming, is not merely a technical problem. It is an output of philosophy and Civilization OS.

## 2. Philosophy Determines the Direction of Civilization

Civilization is not determined by tools alone.

Civilization is determined by what those tools are used for.

Fire can cook food, provide warmth, burn forests, or become a weapon.

AI can share knowledge, enable surveillance, support education, amplify war, or tune civilization.

The same technology produces different civilizations depending on the philosophy it is connected to.

```text
Philosophy
↓
Values
↓
Institutions
↓
Use of science and technology
↓
Economic structure
↓
Urban structure
↓
Consequences for environment, society, and future generations
```

Therefore, the deepest layer of Civilization OS is not technology, but philosophy.

## 3. Characteristics of the Current Civilization OS

Much of modern civilization has developed through the following structure:

```text
Dualism
Linear growth
Short-term profit
Mass production
Mass consumption
Mass disposal
Domination of nature
Competition for resources
Division between us and not-us
```

In this Civilization OS, nature is not treated as a circulating biosphere, but as an external resource.

Forests become timber. Soil becomes a production device. Oceans become fishing grounds and transport routes. The atmosphere becomes a dumping space. Humans become labor and consumers.

As a result, civilization appears to advance while destroying its own survival foundations.

```text
Forest loss
Soil degradation
Water-cycle weakening
Ocean circulation weakening
Organic matter circulation disruption
Urban heat accumulation
CO₂ emissions
Global warming
```

This is not accidental.

If Civilization OS is linear, its outputs also become linear.

Extract, burn, produce, consume, discard.

At the end of that sequence, incompatibility with Earth as a circulating planet appears.

## 4. Division Created by Dualism

Dualism divides the world into opposing pairs.

Good and evil. Justice and enemy. Humanity and nature. Us and them. The ruler and the ruled.

This can simplify judgment, but it also easily fragments the world.

If nature is placed outside humanity, nature becomes an object of domination.

If others are placed outside “us,” exclusion replaces dialogue.

If different faiths, peoples, nations, and philosophies are treated not as partners in coexistence but as enemies, civilization continues to generate conflict.

Many problems of modern civilization are rooted in this dualistic division before they are rooted in technology.

---

## 5. A Civilization OS Based on Wa, Harmony

Prince Shotoku’s Seventeen-Article Constitution contains the famous phrase:

> Harmony is to be valued, and needless opposition is to be avoided.

This should not be read merely as a Japanese moral teaching.

As a Civilization OS, it is a basic principle for directing society toward harmony rather than conflict.

Wa does not mean hiding disagreement.

Wa means not erasing differences, not absolutizing conflict, and repositioning different elements toward harmony of the whole.

It means not opposing natural law, not placing humanity and nature in conflict, not separating the individual from the whole, and using technology for complementarity rather than domination.

If this principle had been placed at the center of Civilization OS, the use of science and technology would have been different.

---

## 6. The Origin of Religion Was Also Civilization OS

Originally, religion was not merely doctrine or group identity.

At the origin of many religions was a role of restraining conflict, sustaining community, regulating human desire, and connecting human society with natural or cosmic order.

In that sense, religion also functioned as an operating system that restrained civilizational runaway.

Religion defined how the world should be seen, what should be considered good, how far desire should be allowed, how people should relate to others, and how humans should relate to nature.

In this sense, the origin of religion was Civilization OS.

However, when such thought is interpreted through dualism, religion can shift from an OS for avoiding conflict into a boundary dividing us from not-us.

An idea originally meant for harmony, moderation, acceptance, and community maintenance can become a tool of domination, exclusion, and conflict.

This is a degradation of Civilization OS.

---

## 7. Acceptance and Sustainable Civilization

A sustainable civilization does not require a philosophy that creates permanent enemies.

It requires a philosophy that does not absolutize conflict, accepts difference, and repositions different elements into the order of the whole.

Many ancient religions and philosophies had, in different forms, this kind of dimension.

Even traditions that speak of conflict between good and evil may also contain interpretations that move toward purification, restoration, acceptance, integration, and reconstruction of order.

If civilization had chosen this direction, technology would have been used not for conquest and expansion, but for harmony and regeneration.

Connected to a Civilization OS of harmony, circulation, and natural complementarity, technology becomes a tool for regenerating Earth.

```text
Linear civilization
```
